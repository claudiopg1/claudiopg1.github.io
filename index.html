I am Claudio Pérez, a physicist and current MSc student in Theoretical Physics at Universidad Arturo Prat, Iquique, Chile. My research lies at the intersection of high energy physics and gravitation, with a focus on the mathematical structures that emerge at the boundaries of spacetime.

My interests center on the asymptotic structure of gauge theories and gravity — a rapidly developing area that connects several deep ideas in modern theoretical physics. This includes flat holography, which explores holographic dualities in asymptotically flat spacetimes; null structures and the geometry of null infinity; and asymptotic symmetries such as BMS and its extensions to gauge theories.

A central thread in this program is the infrared triangle: the remarkable equivalence between asymptotic symmetries, soft theorems in quantum field theory, and gravitational or electromagnetic memory effects. Understanding these connections — and their generalizations — is a guiding motivation for my work.

research
Scalar Field Asymptotic Structure and the Dual Two Form
Study of the asymptotic structure of a scalar field in gravity and its relationship to the dual two-form field. The analysis addresses boundary conditions at null infinity, the associated conserved charges, and the symmetries that emerge in the asymptotic limit — connecting with the BMS symmetry program and its generalizations.
